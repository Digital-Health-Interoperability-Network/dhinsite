THE STANDARDS ORGANIZATION OF NIGERIA (SON) IN COLLABORATION WITH FEDERAL MINISTRY OF HEALTH (FMOH)

32+1 ISO/TC 215 HEALTH INFORMATICS STANDARDS ADOPTED IN 2019

INTEROPERABILITY STANDARDS; TEN (10)
1. ISO/TR 17119: 2015 Health Informatics. Health Informatics profiling
2. ISO/TR 14639-1: 2012 Health Informatics. Capacity based eHealth architecture roadmap.
Part1. Overview of National eHealth Initiatives.
3. ISO/TR 14539-2: 2014 Health Informatics. Part 2. Architectural components and maturity model
4. ISO/TS 13131: 2014 Health Informatics. Telehealth services. Quality planning guidelines.
5. ISO/TS 17522: 2015 Health Informatics. Provision for health application on mobile /smart devices
6. ISO 82308: 2011 Health Informatics. Requirements for an electronic health record architecture.
7. ISO 27789: 2013 Health Informatics. Audit trails for electronic health records.
8. ISO/TR 17975: 2015 Health Informatics. Principle and data requirements for consent in the collection use or disclosure of personal health information
9. ISO/TR 17791: 2013 Health Informatics. Guidance on standards for enabling safety in health software.
10. IEC 82304 – 1: 2016 Health Software. Part 1. General requirements for product safety.
TECHNICAL INTEROPERABILITY STANDARDS; Sixteen (16)
1. ISO/TS 14441: 2013 Health Informatics. Security and privacy requirement of HER systems for use in conformity assessment.
2. ISO 13606-1: 2008 Health Informatics. Electronic Health Record communication. Part 1. Reference Model.
3. ISO 13606-2: 2008 Health Informatics. Part 2. Archetype interchange specification.
4. ISO 13606-5: 2010 Health Informatics. Part 5. Interface specification.
5. ISO/TR 11636: 2009 Health Informatics. Dynamic on demand virtual private network for health information infrastructure.
6. ISO 17090-1: 2013 Health Informatics. Public key infrastructure. Part 1. Overview of digital certificate service.
7. ISO 17090-2: 2015 Health Informatics. Public Key Infrastructure. Part 2. Certificate Profile.
8. ISO 17090-3: 2008 Health Informatics. Public Key Infrastructure Part 3. Policy management of certification authority.
9. ISO/TR 16056-1: 2004 Health Informatics. Interoperability of telehealth systems and networks. Part 1. Introduction and definitions.
10. ISO/TR 18307: 2001 Health Informatics. Interoperability and compatibility in messaging and communication standards. Key Characteristics.
11. ISO/TR 18638: 2017 Health Informatics. Guidance on health informatics privacy education in healthcare organisation.
12. ISO/TS 21547: 2010 Health Informatics. Security requirement for archiving of electronic health records. Principles.
13. ISO/TR 21548: 2010 Health Informatics. Security requirement for archiving of electronic health records. Guidelines.
14. ISO 27799: 2016 Health Informatics. Information security management in health using ISO/IEC 27002.
15. ISO 25237: 2017 Health Informatics. Pseudonymization.
16. ISO/TR 16056-2; 2004 Health Informatics. Interoperability telehealth systems and networks. Part 2. Real Time Systems. (Modified Adoption)
SEMANTIC INTEROPERABILITY (CONTENT) STANDARDS; SIX (6)
1. ISO 17523: 2016 Health Informatics. Requirements for Electronics Prescriptions.
2. ISO/TR 12300: 2014 Health Informatics. Principles of mapping between terminological Systems.
3. ISO/TR 12310: 2015 Health Informatics. Principles and Guidelines for the measurement of conformance in implementation of terminological systems.
4. ISO/TR 20514: 2015 Health Informatics. Electronic Health Records. Definitions, Scope and Context.
5. ISO/TR 13972: 2015 Health Informatics. Detailed Clinical Models. Characteristics and Processes.
6. ISO/TS Health Informatics. Development of terms and definitions for health informatics glossaries.
